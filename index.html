#womensrugby

Branding

The Story

No Pain, No Gain. This is every athletes mantra as they brush off an injury and hit the field again. No where is this more true than in Women's Rugby. The heart of these women for their sport is incredibly inspiring. They sacrifice their bodies, their time and their money in order to play the sport they love.

The Problem

Rugby as a sport is breaking ground world wide. However, as with all sports the women’s division remains a sub category. Possibly the most dedicated people I know, the women I play with are the most inspiring people I have ever worked with. On or off the field. I wanted to shine a light on their heart for the game and how women have impacted Rugby.

The Solution

Social Media is a daily source of inspiration and information. Hashtags have become a way to connect vast amounts of content and synthesize topics. Keeping this in mind I designed a series of motivational posters under the hashtag "womensrugby". This movement can be transferred to a broad stream of media outlets. The quotes are easily applicable to things like apparel, and merchandise.

Tools
  • Photoshop
  • Calligraphy, hand drawn
  • Pop Cap pen, India Ink
  • Shot with D7000

Merry Christmas from Target

Branding

The Story

I love Target. It's kind of lame but it is quickly becoming one of my favourite stores. They have just about everything a person could immediatly need. It's the perfect store for meandering if you have time to kill. The aisle's are wide, bright and clean and the staff doesn't tend to bug you to much.

The Problem

Target Canada depends to much on their status in the U.S to appeal to their new clientel. Unfortunatley they just don't offer the same value and their market is different. In Canada, winter isn't just a season but a way of life that must be embraced. Target needs to step into that market in order to be successful in the Great North.

The Solution

The Merry Christmas campaign was created. Using Target's existing colours but adding a homey element with creative type they introduced their winter collection. Playing off of old Christmas songs, and lines like "Baby it's cold outsdie," they invited clientele in to experience the warmth of the Target Shopping experience. Free cocoa upon arrival and blissful warmth of a store's interior.

Tools
  • Photoshop
  • Calligraphy, hand drawn
  • Pop Cap pen, India Ink
  • Shot with D7000

Eat

App Design

The Story

Your wife, mother, father, siter, brother, or partner walks into the room. There you are, working contentedly on whatever happens to be in front of you. Suddenly, with one little question they have utterly disturbed your peace of mind and leave you longing for the simpler moments before they walked into the room. What is this question? Read on.

The Problem

Everyone is familiar with the age old question, "what would you like to eat?" Some people have an immediate answer, but most are plagued with indecision or indifference. Food in front of them would be nice, how it gets there is of secondary interest.

The Solution

An app of course! Because in this digital age your phone should be able to do everything, including deciding what you are going to eat. Eat, is recipe collective. Users can add their own recipes, or search through a pre-loaded collection of declicious meals. However the app's primary feature is it's random idea generator. Based on the time of day it will provide you with scrumptios meal ideas you can cook at home.

MyChurch Media

Typography/Graphics

The Story

MyChurch is a young church growing under the guidance of pastors Caleb and Julie Davidson. They and their leadership team have a strong belief in the power of media to deliver a message. Their creative team is huge part of what they do within their culture. I recently joined their graphics team, with a strong focus on providing graphics for events.

The Problem

MyChurch is typically focused on reaching university aged students. People between 18 and 25 make up the majority of the church body. Information and content has to be brief, eye-catching and direct. It also needs to be appealing to those beyond the church.

The Solution

Instagraphics were a quick, powerful solution. They were easy to update, tag and share across multiple platforms and with multiple generations. I designed several versions of graphics for a variety of events. These were released each week and continuously require updating and redesign.

  • Adobe Illustrator

  • Multiple Fonts

Sisterhood

Branding

The Story

A passion project that started with Julie Davidson, a pastor at MyChurch, Sisterhood is a movement designed to inspire and empower women. City wide Sisterhood conferences are held 3-4 times a year. Each conference is given it’s own label and branding identity specific to the event.

The Problem

I was approached to design a logo type for the Sisterhood brand as well as a look for the next conference, “United”. It needed to be colourful and girly without being cliche. They wanted something that would be applicable across multiple platforms, including video.

The Solution

Given the organic and inspirational focus of Sisterhood, the hand lettered logotype seemed appropriate. Strong but feminine it would be easy to partner with alternate brand looks. The United needed to have some of the same organic feel. A watercolour effect and some photoshop brushes helped to customize the standard text and tie in the Sisterhood logo.

Tools
  • Brush Pen
  • Adobe Illustrator
  • Photoshop
  • Adobe InDesign

365 Series

Typography

The Story

Instagram and tumblr are hugely popular platforms for designers. I was inspired by artists who used these networks to share their work and create amazing series. I decided to take the plunge and do the same. A 365 day lettering projects seemed to be the perfect blend of two of my passions. Typography and photography.

The Problem

In the past couple of years I find I often lose my excitement for my work in the midst of homework assignments. These were projects I had to do and they were the same ones everyone else was doing. I needed something to help me grow my skill set, and regenerate my creative energies. Embarking on this project has been a growing experience and just a lot of fun.

The Solution

Perusing pinterest, instagram and tumblr provided a wealth of inspiration for experimental fonts and letter choices. Inspiration was everywhere, in movies, the credits from old disney classics and in books and magazines. The series has grown, and is deepening as a collection of words that inspire me, make me laugh or just look really cool. Eventually prints and cards would be an awesome way to expand and experiment.

Tools

  • Adobe Illustrator
  • Sketchbook
  • Adobe Photoshop

Photography

Personal

The Story

I love photography, I love capturing moments, or objects that remind me of other moments. I love people’s faces and the stories they tell. Perfect light, shadows, the little details that make ordinary objects beautiful.

The Problem

I had albums and albums of photos with blurry faces, bad light, awkwardly posed subjects and out of focus objects. These images failed to capture the joy and life being lived beyond the frame of the camera. I wanted to be able to take picture that captured joy, humour, and a random assortment of other emotions.

The Solution

I needed to learn to take a decent photograph, and learn how to edit. I’ve taken hundreds and hundreds of photographs. Some worth sharing and some that should never be seen by other people. I offered to do shoots for friends and I took my camera with me everywhere. I read articles, books and lists on tips, tricks and techniues. I collected images I like and tried to copy them, icked artists I liked and mimicked their style.

The Secret Life of Walter Mitty

Poster Design

The Story

The Secret Life of Walter Mitty, is an amazing movie. Totally quirky and definitely awesome it combines music, typography, humour, and cinematography in a heart warming blend of self discovery and exploration. The story line is slightly ridiculous, but it speaks to the explorer's heart in me.

The Problem

“Design a movie poster” That was it, that was the assignment. It could be any movie. The only guide lines were, it had to be simple and reduce the movie to it’s basic components. I went through 8 different movies before I settled on The Secret Life of Walter Mitty.

The Solution

Recently my work has been very much photography based. I wanted to expand and try something new. I took an illustrative approach and broke down one of the pivotal scenes in the movie. The scene is the first time that Walter Mitty does something adventurous. It wasn’t the most sensible leap, but he jumped and that pretty much summed up the movie. I added some texture in photoshop, and overlayed a map look to show the travel that takes place in the movive.

Luke's Diner

Branding

The Story

There are three things I really like. Food, design and Netflix. One of my recent show's involved a diner called Luke's. It's tacky and cliche and exactly the sort of place you wished really existed. At one point the in the show the diner was destroyed and required a remodel. They didn't change anything about it in the show but it got me thinking about how would I rebrand it?

The Problem

The original diner look was very cliche, it had a red and yellow sign in the shape of a coffee cup with the word Luke's on it. In recent years, cafe's and diners have been getting updated looks that are very modern and trendy. I wanted to do something similar in order to keep it relevant but something retained a bit of the diner character.

The Solution

The design concept I came up with took a patriotic turn with a red, white and blue colour theme. It seemed appropriate considering the cultural background of diner's. A simple logo became the focal point. Quirky, original icons were made to match, a collection of cooking utensils commonly found in any kitchen added detail and provided the building blocks for multiple elements. Simply rearranging the icons into various patterns could be transferred to takeout labels and containers, menu's, glass ware, paper ware and other branding materials.
