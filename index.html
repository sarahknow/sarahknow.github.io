Who Am I?

I am a west coast girl, open spaces and mountain ranges make me happy. I love people especially the ones who are insanely passionate about what they do. I’m a designer, explorer, connector and athlete. I’m crazy about life and plan to live a remarkable one. So far the experience has been pretty awesome.

What am I doing?

I’m doing what I’m good at. Math and science didn’t make sense but negative space did. Typography and photography are the elements I most love to work with, but ultimately I want to tell stories and help to create experiences.

Why am I doing it?

Because design is about people. People who love to create, to live, to explore. People who are passionate about life, beauty and curiosity. These are the people I want to work with, learn from and inspire.

#womensrugby

Branding

The Story

No Pain, No Gain. This is every athletes motivation as they brush off an injury and hit the field running. No where is this more true than in Women's Rugby. The heart of these women for their sport is incredibly inspiring. They sacrifice their bodies, their time and their money in order to play the sport they love.

The Problem

Rugby as a sport is breaking ground world wide. However, as with all sports the women’s division remains a sub category. Possibly the most dedicated people I know, the women I play with are some of the most inspiring people I have worked with. On or off the field. I wanted to shine a light on their heart for the game and how women have impacted the sport.

The Solution

Social Media is a daily source of inspiration and information. Hashtags have become a way to connect vast amounts of content and synthesize topics. Keeping this in mind I designed a series of motivational posters under the #womensrugby. This can be transferred to a broad stream of media and the quotes are easily applicable to things like apparel, and merchandise.

Tools
  • Photoshop
  • Calligraphy, hand drawn
  • Pop Cap pen, India Ink
  • Shot with D7000

Speak, We're Listening

Print Design

The Story

The power of language is dramatic. Words have an unquestionable impact on our lives. I was super inspired by a trailer done by Hillsong for their 2015 conference. The words really hit home and when challenged by a professor to do a type layout I knew I had to set these words to paper.

The Problem

How to set such an emotionally, visually charged piece to paper? How to transfer something so dynamic to a static medium? The layout had to incorporate images and text and should ultimately be usable as a coffee table book or conversation starter.

The Solution

When designing the book, in order to create a sense of power and rhythm I used scale to break up the text. Negative space and creative type setting also helped to demonstrate flow and keep things dynamic. I kept the colour pallet cool, using images of nature to help convey the topic.

Tools
  • Images, Shot with D7000
  • Adobe InDesign
  • Adobe Photoshop

TMyChurch Media

Typography/Graphics

The Story

MyChurch is a start up church under Caleb and Julie Davidson. They and their leadership team have a strong belief in the power of media to deliver a message. Their creative team is huge part of what they do within their culture. I recently joined their graphics team, with a strong focus on providing instagram graphics for events.

The Problem

MyChurch is typically focused on reaching university aged students, people between 18 and 25 make up the majority of the church. Information and content has to be brief, eye-catching and direct. It also needs to be appealing to those beyond the church.

The Solution

Instagraphics were a quick, powerful solution. They were easy to update, tag and share across multiple platforms and with multiple generations. I designed several versions of graphics for a variety of events, these were released each week and continuously require updating and redesign.

Tools
  • Adobe Photoshop
  • Adobe Illustrator
  • Photography, Stock Images
  • Multiple Fonts

Sisterhood

Branding

The Story

A passion project that started with Julie Davidson, a pastor at MyChurch, Sisterhood is a movement designed to inspire and empower women. 3-4 times a year Sisterhood conferences are held. Each conference is given it’s own label and branding specific to the event.

The Problem

I was approached to design a logo type for the Sisterhood brand as well as a look for the next conference, “United”. It needed to be colourful and girly without being cliche. They wanted something that would be applicable across multiple platforms, including video.

The Solution

Given the organic and inspirational focus of Sisterhood, the hand lettered logotype seemed appropriate. Strong but feminine it would be easy to partner with alternate brand looks. The United needed to have some of the same organic feel. A watercolour effect and some photoshop brushes helped to customize the standard text and tie in the Sisterhood logo.

Tools
  • Brush Pen
  • Adobe Illustrator
  • Photoshop
  • Adobe InDesign

365 Series

Typography

The Story

Instagram and tumblr are hugely popular platforms for designers. Inspired by artists who had begun series of works using these networks to share their pieces I decided to take the plunge and do the same. Typography fascinates me as does photography and this seemed the perfect blending of both.

The Problem

I found would often get bogged down by homework assignments. Projects I had to do, the same ones everyone else was doing. I needed something to help me grow my skill set, and regenerate my creative energies. Embarking on this project has been a growing experience and just a lot of fun.

The Solution

Perusing pinterest, instagram and tumblr provided a wealth of inspiration for experimental fonts and letter choices. Old movies and their credit screens provided more, the result is a deepening collection of words that inspire me, make me laugh or just look really cool. Eventually prints and cards would be an awesome way to expand and experiment.

Tools
  • Various Pens
  • Adobe Illustrator
  • Sketchbook
  • Adobe Photoshop

Photography

Personal

The Story

I love photography, I love capturing moments, or objects that remind me of other moments. People’s faces and the stories they tell. Perfect light, shadows, the little details that make ordinary objects beautiful.

The Problem

Albums and albums of photos with blurry faces, bad light, posed people and out of focus objects. These images failed to capture the joy and life being lived beyond the frame of the camera. Folders of clutter on desktops and empty walls begging to be filled with colour.

The Solution

Learn to take a decent photograph, and learn how to edit. I’ve taken hundreds and hundreds of photographs. Some worth sharing and some that should not see anything but the trash. Shooting for friends allowed me to broaden my skill set and expand the practical aspect.

Tools
  • Nikon D7000
  • 18-55mm lens
  • 55mm 1.4f Prime
  • Photoshop

The Secret Life of Walter Mitty

Poster Design

The Story

The Secret Life of Walter Mitty, that movie is amazing. Totally quirky and definitely awesome it combines music, typography, humour, and cinematography in a heart warming blend of self discovery and exploration. The story line is slightly ridiculous, but it speaks to the adventurer in me.

The Problem

“Design a movie poster” That was it, that was the assignment. It could be any movie but it had to be simple and break down the movie to it’s basic components. I went through 8 different movies before I settled on The Secret Life of Walter Mitty.

The Solution

Recently my work has been very much photography based and I wanted to expand and try something new. I took an illustrative approach and broke down one of the pivotal scenes in the movie. The scene is the first time that Walter Mitty does something adventurous. It wasn’t the most sensible leap, but he jumped and that pretty much summed up the movie.

The Idea
  • Adobe Illustrator
  • Adobe Photoshop
  • Sketchbook

Spicely Organic

Branding and Typography

The Story

There are these beautiful old ceramic jars in my cupboard. They are used to store spices and random other things, but they have such a unique look I always wanted to do something with them. Packaging has always interested me as well, I’ll buy things just because they look cool, and I knew these would be the perfect container to work with.

The Problem

Spicely Organic is a shop in San Fransico that is committed to providing real products. Free of fillers, sweeteners, MSG and GMO’s. They have received their 100% Organic Certification and are on their way to becoming Kosher, Vegan and Gluten Free Products. The logo is clean and simple and understated. For a product that is so earthy their logo seemed almost to clean.

The Solution

Inspired by the rich aroma and flavour of natural herbs and spices, this rebranding project begged for bold typography and contrast. A little bit of texture helped to set things off. Strong angles and bold lines also seemed appropriate.

The Idea
  • Illustrator
  • Brush Pen
  • Photoshop
